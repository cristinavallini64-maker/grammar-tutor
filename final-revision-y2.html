Look at the text in each question. What does it say? Choose the correct letter A, B or C.
SPORTS CENTRE — TENNIS COURTS You can play without booking, but if more than two people are waiting, you must give up the court after 30 minutes.
1. What does the notice tell players?
  • A) Players always have to leave the court after half an hour.
  • B) Players may have to stop playing if other people want the court.
  • C) Players must book the court in advance if they want to play.
Soluzione: B. L'obbligo (must give up) è condizionale: scatta solo if more than two people are waiting. A è la trappola: trasforma l'obbligo condizionale in uno assoluto (always) — falso, perché senza fila si può restare. C contraddice You can play without booking.
Hi Tom, I tried to buy the cinema tickets online but the website wasn't working. Could you get them when you arrive? I'll pay you back tonight. Lucy
2. Why is Lucy writing to Tom?
  • A) She wants Tom to buy the tickets because she could not.
  • B) She is telling Tom that she has already bought the tickets.

Soluzione: A. I tried to buy … but the website wasn't working = il tentativo è fallito, quindi B è falso (non li ha comprati). C inverte i ruoli: è Lucy che pagherà Tom (I'll pay you back), non il contrario.

Soluzione: C. La zona silenziosa non sparisce, è stata spostata di piano (moved upstairs) — quindi B («nessun posto tranquillo») è la trappola. A contraddice will stay open.
Mum, Jake's already left for football, so I've gone to the shopping centre on my own. Don't worry about lunch — I'll get something there. Back by 4. Sam
4. What do we learn from Sam's message?
  • A) Sam has gone to play football with Jake.

Soluzione: B. Don't worry about lunch — I'll get something there significa che mangerà fuori, quindi C è falsa. A confonde i due soggetti: è Jake che è andato a calcio, Sam è andato al centro commerciale (on my own).
SCHOOL TRIP — CHANGE OF PLAN The museum is fully booked on Friday, so we will go there on Thursday instead. Friday's visit to the science park will not change.
5. What has changed about the school trip?
  • A) the day of the museum visit
  • B) the day of the science park visit
  • C) the place the students will visit on Friday
Soluzione: A. Solo il museo si sposta (da venerdì a giovedì). B e C sono trappole: il science park resta a venerdì immutato (will not change), quindi né il giorno né il luogo del venerdì cambiano.

Riepilogo Part 1 — Strutture testate:

1: Obbligo condizionale (must dentro un if) vs obbligo assoluto.

2: tried to + infinito (tentativo fallito); inversione soggetto/oggetto come trappola.

3: Comparativo noisier; present perfect passivo (has been moved).

4: have gone; confusione tra due soggetti come trappola.

5: Futuro con will; individuare cosa cambia e cosa no.

My First Cycling Holiday
by Emma Foster, aged 15

When my parents first suggested a cycling holiday, I wasn't keen at all. I had imagined long, boring days on quiet country roads, and I almost said no. My older brother Leo, on the other hand, couldn't stop talking about it for weeks. In the end I agreed to go, mostly because all my friends were away that summer and staying at home alone seemed even worse.

We didn't choose the destination ourselves. My dad works for a travel company, and one of his colleagues had organised the whole trip as a favour. We were simply told the name of the village — Greenholme — the day before we left. I had never heard of it, and to be honest I didn't expect much.

The first morning was harder than anything I had imagined. The hills went on forever, and after two hours my legs were aching so badly that I had to stop. Leo, who is two years older and far stronger than me, kept cycling ahead and then waiting at the top with a big smile. I won't pretend I was grateful for that smile. By lunchtime I had decided that the whole week was going to be a disaster.

Things changed in the afternoon. We reached the top of a long climb, and suddenly the sea appeared below us. None of the photos my dad's colleague had shown us came close to the real thing. For the first time I understood why Leo had been so excited, and I found myself wanting to go faster rather than slower.

On the third day, something happened that I still can't fully explain. We had stopped by a lake to rest when a young woman cycled past, then turned around and came back. She said she had been watching me on the hills the day before and that I had a natural cycling style. I knew she was probably just being kind, but I rode the rest of that day feeling as if I could climb any mountain in the world.

We are already planning next summer's trip. My mum keeps reminding me how I complained before we left, and I always change the subject. Some things are better left unsaid — especially when your family is right and you were wrong.

Riepilogo Part 3 — Come ragionare:

In questa parte la risposta non è quasi mai scritta con le stesse parole della frase: va dedotta. Cerca il senso, non le parole uguali.

Trappole tipiche: attribuire a Emma sentimenti che erano di Leo (11, 14); litote / negazione indiretta (I won't pretend I was grateful = non era grato, 14); confondere certezza e dubbio (18).

Strutture coinvolte: past perfect, was going to, comparativi, present perfect, tried to, defining relative clauses.

A Film Worth Seeing
Essay by Daniel Carter, aged 14

I have always been a huge fan of adventure films, so when my friends asked me to go to the cinema last weekend, I immediately said yes. The film we chose was a new science fiction movie called Distant Skies. I had read several reviews before going, and most of them were positive, although a few critics complained that the story was too slow at the start. I wanted to find out for myself whether they were right.

The cinema itself has changed a lot since I last went. The company that owns it has spent a lot of money making the seats more comfortable and the screen much bigger than before. You don't have to arrive early to get a good seat any more, although I still think you should, because the best places fill up quickly. I have visited many cinemas in different towns, but this is certainly the most modern one I have ever been to.

As for the film, I have to be honest. The first twenty minutes were indeed quite slow, and I began to wonder if the critics had been right. However, once the main character started his journey across the galaxy, the action never stopped. The special effects were the best I have ever seen, and the actress who plays the scientist gives a brilliant performance. I won't tell you how the film ends, because you should discover that yourself.

If I had to find a problem, I would say the film is a little too long. It lasts almost three hours, and by the end I was feeling tired. I also felt there weren't as many funny moments as in the director's earlier films. Even so, I enjoyed it far more than I had expected. Tomorrow I am meeting the same friends to see another film, and if it is half as good as this one, we will have a great afternoon.

21. What is Daniel trying to do in his essay?
  • A) explain how cinema tickets have become cheaper
  • B) complain about the science fiction films of today
  • C) give a balanced opinion of a film he has seen
  • D) describe how a film should be made
Soluzione: C. Daniel elenca aspetti positivi (effetti speciali, recitazione) e negativi (inizio lento, durata eccessiva): è un giudizio equilibrato. Non parla di prezzi (A) né critica tutto il genere (B).
22. What can a reader find out from Daniel's essay?
  • A) what the critics' main complaint about the film was
  • B) how the film Distant Skies ends
  • C) why the film has the title Distant Skies
  • D) whether he thinks the cinema is a good one
Soluzione: D. Daniel dà un giudizio chiaro sul cinema (the most modern one I have ever been to). A è la trappola: i critici sono citati, ma Daniel non dice qual era la loro lamentela — dice solo che alcuni la trovavano lenta. B e C: il testo afferma che il finale e il titolo non li rivelerà / non li conosce, quindi il lettore non lo «scopre».
23. What does Daniel tell us about the cinema?
  • A) It has been improved since his last visit.
  • B) Daniel advises against bothering to arrive early.
  • C) The screen is smaller than it used to be.
  • D) It is the only cinema Daniel has ever visited.
Soluzione: A. Present perfect has spent … making the seats more comfortable = il cinema è stato migliorato. B è la trappola: Daniel dice you don't have to arrive early, ma poi consiglia comunque di farlo (I still think you should) — quindi non «sconsiglia». D forza: ne ha visti molti (I have visited many cinemas).
24. What is one problem with the film, according to Daniel?
  • A) The opening scenes move too quickly.
  • B) It goes on for too long.
  • C) The main actress performs badly.
  • D) It is not as serious as the director's earlier films.
Soluzione: B. The film is a little too long … almost three hours (too + adjective). A inverte: l'inizio è lento, non veloce. D è vera come fatto (è meno seria… anzi più seria dei precedenti) ma Daniel non la presenta come un difetto: la domanda chiede un problema.
25. Which of these might appear in a magazine review of the film?
  • A) The action never slows down, so be prepared for excitement from the very first minute.
  • B) This is the funniest film the director has ever made, with jokes from start to finish.
  • C) The story takes a while to begin, but once it does, the effects and acting make it well worth watching.
  • D) A short, simple film that you will easily forget by the time you leave the cinema.
Soluzione: C. Riassume fedelmente il testo: inizio lento (the first twenty minutes were slow), poi effetti e recitazione eccellenti. A sbaglia (l'inizio è lento), B sbaglia (meno momenti divertenti dei film precedenti), D sbaglia (il film è lungo, non corto).

The Photograph on the Wall

There is an old black-and-white photograph on the wall of my grandmother's kitchen. It shows a young woman standing by the sea, and for years I thought it was just a picture she had bought somewhere. Last month, however, my grandmother finally told me the truth. The woman in the photograph actually her, taken more than fifty years ago.

She explained that, as a teenager, she had wanted a swimmer, and that she used to train in the sea every morning before school. the water was freezing and her family thought she was crazy, she never missed a single day. By the age of sixteen, she several local competitions.

What surprised me most was learning why she stopped. She told me she was much than most of the other swimmers, but there simply weren't opportunities for girls in her village at that time. Instead of complaining, she decided younger children, and she did that for nearly thirty years.

“I it,” she said, looking at the photograph. “If you ever find something you love, you share it with other people.” I have thought about her words ever since.

Spiegazioni Part 5:

26 [C] that: defining relative clause su una cosa (a picture). That sostituisce which; who e whose non valgono per un oggetto. La trappola è where, ma il referente è il quadro, non un luogo.

27 [B] was: tutta la frase è al passato (taken more than fifty years ago): la donna nella foto era lei. L'indizio è lontano, alla fine della frase.

28 [A] to become: dopo want (had wanted) si usa l'infinito con to.

29 [D] Although: la frase oppone due idee (acqua gelida / non saltava un giorno): serve un connettore di contrasto. Because darebbe una causa, senza senso qui.

30 [B] had won: past perfect: by the age of sixteen indica un risultato già raggiunto entro un momento del passato.

31 [A] faster: comparativo + than (l'indizio than arriva dopo lo spazio). Much rinforza il comparativo, non il superlativo.

32 [C] enough: weren't enough opportunities = non c'erano abbastanza occasioni. Too much e a lot non reggono qui un plurale numerabile con la negazione.

33 [D] to train: dopo decide si usa l'infinito con to.

34 [A] have never regretted: present perfect con never: esperienza che dal passato continua a valere nel presente (lo dice oggi guardando la foto).

35 [B] should: consiglio. È un first conditional (If you ever find …): nella principale ci vuole un modale + base form. Must to è errato (i modali non vogliono to).
