Part 1 - Short Messages
Look at the text in each question. What does it say? Choose the correct letter A, B or C.
I usually do my homework on Friday afternoon because I love going to the cinema on Saturdays.
What does the person do?
  • A) They always go to the cinema on Fridays.
  • B) They do their homework at the cinema.
  • C) They normally finish their schoolwork before Saturday.
Spiegazione: "Normally" è sinonimo di "usually". Se la persona svolge i compiti di venerdì pomeriggio ("Friday afternoon"), significa che li termina prima di sabato ("before Saturday").
School Cafeteria: We only have a few biscuits left. If you want fruit, there are a lot of apples.
What can students buy at the cafeteria?
  • A) Plenty of apples but not many biscuits.
  • B) As many biscuits as they want.
  • C) A little bit of apple juice.
Spiegazione: "A lot of" corrisponde a "plenty of" (molte), mentre "a few" significa "not many" (poche, limitate).
Hi Alex, I can't stand playing video games alone. Do you want to meet at the park later?
What is the writer's opinion?
  • A) They love playing video games by themselves.
  • B) They prefer meeting friends to playing video games alone.
  • C) They hate going to the park.
Spiegazione: La locuzione "I can't stand" esprime forte antipatia o intolleranza; pertanto, la persona preferisce l'alternativa di incontrarsi fuori con gli amici.
To all students: The music class is meeting outside the theatre tomorrow at 8 p.m. for the concert.
What is happening tomorrow evening?
  • A) The music class is going to an event together.
  • B) Students are having a regular music lesson at school.
  • C) The theatre is closing at 8 p.m.
Spiegazione: Il presente progressivo "is meeting" indica una pianificazione futura stabilita. Presenziare a un concerto equivale a partecipare collettivamente a un evento ("an event").
Hi Mum, I went to the shop but I didn't buy any chocolate because it was too expensive.
What happened at the shop?
  • A) The person bought some expensive chocolate.
  • B) The person did not go to the shop.
  • C) The person left the shop without chocolate.
Spiegazione: La forma negativa "didn't buy" certifica che l'azione dell'acquisto non è stata finalizzata a causa del prezzo eccessivo.

Jack was a remarkably hard-working student who usually spent the entirety of his weekends inside his family home. He generally loved playing complex video games alone or watching long TV series in his bedroom. He rarely went out into the town centre because he genuinely did not enjoy noisy or crowded places, preferring the quiet comfort of his personal space.

However, last weekend, a sudden change occurred. His classmates sent him a message inviting him to a special cultural event. A popular local artist was showing a unique collection of original stone sculptures at the modern art gallery. After the exhibition, the group also planned to walk to the historic theatre to attend a live music concert. Because Jack was incredibly keen on art and history, he decided to break his normal habits and join them for the afternoon.

The students all met outside the main entrance of the gallery at exactly 4:30 p.m., taking advantage of their usual afternoon break from studying. The exhibition was fascinating, and before leaving, Jack wanted to buy a small souvenir. He walked into the busy gallery shop and noticed some beautiful black and purple T-shirts. Unfortunately, they were far too expensive, and he realised he did not have enough money in his pocket for clothes. Instead of leaving empty-handed, he just bought a few small postcards of the sculptures to remember the day, which were much cheaper.

As the sun went down, the evening began, and the group moved to the town theatre for the highly anticipated concert. At first, Jack felt very uncomfortable and did not like the show at all; the music was far too loud for his taste, and there were too many people shouting around him. However, things changed drastically during the second part of the performance. The band stopped using electric instruments and played some traditional acoustic songs instead. Afterwards, feeling exhausted, they went to a nearby cafeteria. Jack usually hates fast food and avoids it at all costs, but on this occasion, he was absolutely starving. He made a temporary exception to his strict diet and ordered some grilled fish and a glass of fresh orange juice. Meanwhile, his classmates sat at the table eating chocolate biscuits and drinking sweet milkshakes. It was a completely new experience for him. Tomorrow morning, Jack is meeting his friends again at the local sports centre to play a long match of tennis, and he feels exceptionally happy about this active new routine.

An honest review of the new town theatre
Essay by Alex Green, aged 15

I’ve always been a huge fan of live performances and history, so I was incredibly excited when the town council finally opened the new Millennium Theatre last week. My classmates and I immediately bought tickets for the opening music concert. Before the building was completed, many people complained that it was too expensive to build, but in my opinion, it is a magnificent addition to our town.

All the modern theatres I’ve visited recently have felt cold, but this one is different. The architect, who has designed lots of other commercial spaces, made this theatre look like an old 19th-century opera house. Even though the seats are a little small and there isn't enough space for long legs, it’s the most beautiful venue in the area. I did wonder at first whether the sound would be clear enough near the back rows—but the acoustics were absolutely perfect. The young actress who presented the show gave a fantastic performance too, and I loved the traditional costumes she wore.

The theatre management probably had a hard job choosing a performance that would please everyone. The show started with an energetic rock band, which provided plenty of excitement right at the beginning, but unfortunately, I found the music a bit too loud and difficult to enjoy in places. It also seemed to be over very quickly—the first part lasted under an hour. I also felt there weren't as many traditional acoustic songs as we expected. However, the second half was much more serious and artistic, and I still really enjoyed the experience overall. Tomorrow, my school group is meeting the director backstage to see how the stage machinery works, and I can't wait.

Spiegazioni Linguistiche Part 5:

26 [B]: Costruzione fissa del verbo regolare: decided to do something.

27 [D]: Richiede obbligatoriamente il pronome oggetto immediato (told me), a differenza di said.

28 [A]: Connettore causale (because) per spiegare la motivazione meteorologica.

29 [C]: Struttura quantitativa fissa temporale: a few minutes.

30 [B]: Unico quantificatore compatibile con la preposizione successiva lots of.

31 [D]: Collocazione corretta per indicare sufficienza numerica numerabile: enough players.

32 [A]: Connettore coordinante di logica conseguenza (so = quindi).

33 [C]: Espressione idiomatica fissa al passato remoto: made friends (fare amicizia).

34 [D]: Regola dei verbi di gradimento: don't mind / hate richiedono la forma in -ing.

35 [C]: Present Continuous con valore di futuro programmato coordinato dall'avverbio Tomorrow.
